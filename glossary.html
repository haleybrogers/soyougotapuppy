Glossary

dog training jargon → translated into human. tap for the real definition.

Capturingrewarding your puppy for accidentally being good
Rewarding behaviors the dog offers naturally, without prompting. One of the most powerful training methods.
Marker wordthe "yes!" that tells them THAT was right
A sound (clicker or verbal "yes") that precisely marks the correct moment, followed by a reward.
Positive reinforcementadd something good → behavior increases
Adding a desirable stimulus (food, play, attention) to increase behavior frequency. AKA: bribery that works.
Thresholdthe line between "I'm cool" and "I AM NOT COOL"
The point at which a stimulus becomes too intense for the dog to focus or learn. Work below it.
Shapingrewarding baby steps toward a goal
Reinforcing successive approximations toward a target behavior. Like hot/cold but with kibble.
Luringguiding them with a treat like a puppet
Using a treat to physically guide the dog into position. Fade the lure within 3-5 reps.
Impulse controlteaching them to think before they act
The ability to resist an immediate desire for a better outcome. Basically: adulting.
Desensitizationmaking scary stuff boring with food
Gradual, controlled exposure to a scary thing at low intensity, paired with treats.
Counter-conditioning"that scary thing means cheese now"
Pairing a trigger with something the dog loves to change the emotional response.
Extinction burstit gets worse before it gets better
When you stop rewarding a behavior and they go HARDER before giving up. Like pressing an elevator button 47 times.
Place / mat trainingportable calm — take it anywhere
Teaching them a specific mat = chill zone. Builds duration and distraction tolerance.
Generalizationknowing "sit" in the kitchen but not the park
A skill learned in one context needs practice in new ones to transfer. That's normal.
Enrichmentbrain work so they don't eat your couch
Puzzle toys, sniff walks, frozen Kongs — anything that makes them think instead of destroy.
Managementprevention > correction
Setting up the environment so they can't practice the wrong thing. Baby gates, crates, shoes in closets.
Fear periodwhen the trash can becomes a serial killer
Developmental windows (~8-10 weeks and ~6-14 months) where they're extra sensitive. Don't force them through it.
Dominance theorydebunked. let it go.
That thing your uncle brings up at Thanksgiving. Based on flawed 1930s wolf studies. Even the original researcher said he was wrong.
