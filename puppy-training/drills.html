Training drills

Quick exercises. 2-5 minutes each. Do them daily.

🎯
The Recall Game
All ages ✧ 3-5 min ✧ Meals
  1. Grab a handful of kibble from their meal
  2. Say "GET IT" and throw a piece
  3. When they look back, say "COME"
  4. When they come back, celebrate and throw another
  5. Repeat until meal is done
Do this at every meal. By week 2, they'll come running before you even finish saying the word.
🔄
On/Off Switch
8+ weeks ✧ 2-3 min ✧ Toy + treats
  1. Say "READY??" and play tug or fetch (30 sec)
  2. Say "ENOUGH" and go completely still
  3. Wait for them to settle (sit, lie down, or just stop being wild)
  4. The SECOND they're calm → say "READY??" again
  5. Repeat 3-4 cycles
You're teaching them that calm STARTS the fun again. The faster they settle, the faster they get to play.
✋
Leave It (Levels)
10+ weeks ✧ 2 min ✧ Treats
  1. Level 1: Treat in closed fist. Wait for them to back off. Mark + reward from OTHER hand
  2. Level 2: Treat on floor, cover with hand if they go for it
  3. Level 3: Treat on floor, uncovered. Reward from pocket
  4. Level 4: Drop treat while walking. Reward for ignoring
Only go to the next level when they nail the current one 8/10 times. Don't rush it.
🚪
Doorway Manners
10+ weeks ✧ 1-2 min ✧ Door + leash
  1. Walk to the door with puppy on leash
  2. Reach for the handle — if they get excited, remove hand and wait
  3. Try again. Only open when they're calm(ish)
  4. If they blast through, close door (gently) and reset
  5. Calm puppy = door opens = jackpot treat outside
You're the passive aggressive teacher. Class doesn't start until everyone settles down.
🧘
Capturing Calm
All ages ✧ Ongoing ✧ Kibble in pocket
  1. Carry kibble around the house (yes, like a crazy person)
  2. When puppy lies down on their own → quietly toss them a treat
  3. When puppy sits calmly → treat
  4. When puppy makes eye contact → treat
  5. Don't say anything. Just reward calm whenever you see it.
This is the most powerful drill that doesn't look like training. You're teaching them that chill = the best strategy in life.
📛
Name Game
8+ weeks ✧ 1-2 min ✧ High value treats
  1. Say their name ONE time (don't repeat it)
  2. The instant they look at you → mark ("yes!") + treat
  3. Wait a few seconds, then say their name again
  4. Repeat 10 times
  5. Practice in different rooms and with mild distractions
Their name should mean "look at me, something good is coming." Not "you're in trouble." Never use their name to scold them.
