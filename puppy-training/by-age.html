What's normal by age

So you stop googling "is my puppy broken" at 2am.

🍼 Baby Baby (8-12 weeks)

AKA: the cute-but-clueless phase

What's happening in their brain: EVERYTHING IS NEW. They're in peak socialization mode — their brain is a sponge soaking up every experience.

First fear period happens around 8-10 weeks. A single scary experience can have lasting effects. Be gentle. Don't force them into anything scary.

Focus on:

  • Socialization (people, sounds, surfaces, environments)
  • Crate training + potty schedule
  • Name recognition
  • Hand feeding + recall game
  • Bite inhibition (soft mouth)
  • Handling (touch paws, ears, mouth — with treats)

Don't freak out about:

  • Potty accidents (they literally can't hold it long)
  • Biting everything (they explore with their mouth)
  • Short attention span (normal, keep sessions under 2 min)

🚩 Most common mistakes at this age:

  • Skipping socialization because "they're not fully vaccinated"
  • Not enforcing nap times (overtired = demon mode)
  • Punishing potty accidents instead of supervising better
  • Letting everyone hold/pass around the puppy when puppy is stressed
exercise Very short walks only (5 min per month of age). Let them sniff. Let them explore at their own pace. No marathons.

😈 Teething Gremlin (3-4 months)

AKA: why is everything in their mouth

What's happening: Baby teeth are falling out, adult teeth are coming in. Their mouth hurts. Everything must be chewed.

Focus on:

  • LOTS of appropriate chew options (frozen Kongs, wet washcloths frozen, bully sticks)
  • Continue socialization before the window closes (~14-16 weeks)
  • Basic obedience: sit, down, stay (very short sessions)
  • Start leave it + drop it (you'll need these)
  • Begin loose leash walking practice (short, low-distraction)

Don't freak out about:

  • Biting getting worse (teething pain = more mouthing)
  • Finding tiny teeth on the floor (normal)
  • Chewing intensity peaking at 4-5 months

🚩 Most common mistakes at this age:

  • Thinking socialization window is "later" (it's closing NOW)
  • Not providing enough chew options and then punishing chewing
  • Starting too many commands at once instead of nailing one at a time

🤘 Teenage Dirtbag (4-6 months)

AKA: they know what you want and are choosing not to do it

What's happening: Independence is increasing. They're testing boundaries. They're like a tween who just discovered they have free will.

Focus on:

  • Consistency. This is when they test if the rules are real
  • Recall on a long line (15-30 ft, clipped to harness)
  • Impulse control drills (doorways, leave it, wait)
  • On/off switch practice
  • Place/mat training

Don't freak out about:

  • Selective hearing (they haven't actually forgotten, they're just... choosing)
  • Increased energy (this is peak puppy energy)
  • Regression on previously learned behaviors (stay consistent)

🚩 Most common mistakes at this age:

  • Giving up on recall because "they used to come and now they don't"
  • Letting them off-leash before recall is solid (long line first!)
  • Not increasing mental stimulation as physical exercise increases

🔥 Full Adolescent Chaos (6-12 months)

AKA: the phase where most dogs get surrendered. don't give up.

What's happening: Their brain is literally rewiring. Sexual maturity. A possible second fear period (6-14 months). Everything feels inconsistent.

Focus on:

  • PATIENCE. This is the hardest part. Keep going.
  • Continue all training — don't assume they "know it"
  • Increase mental stimulation (puzzle toys, scent work, training games)
  • Practice in new environments (generalize all skills)
  • Stay calm during the second fear period — don't punish fear
real talk They haven't "forgotten" everything you taught them. Their brain is reorganizing. Like a teenager who suddenly can't remember how to do dishes even though they've done them 100 times. Keep showing up. It clicks eventually.

🎓 Finally a Dog (1-2 years)

AKA: you survived. they survived. everyone survived.

What's happening: Behavioral stabilization. Full emotional maturity (can take up to 2-3 years for large breeds). The foundation you built is paying off.

Keep doing:

  • Training is ongoing. Not a phase. A lifestyle.
  • Mental enrichment daily (puzzle toys, scent work, learning new things)
  • Recall practice (always worth reinforcing)
  • New experiences and socialization don't stop
you did it If you've been consistent through the puppy chaos, you now have a dog who understands how the world works. They know what gets them what they want. And hopefully... it's calm.

Your dog is not the problem. Your inconsistency is.
