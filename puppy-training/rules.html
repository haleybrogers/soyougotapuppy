The only thing you need to understand

Your puppy is constantly asking one question:

"did that work for me?"

That's it. They don't speak English. They don't understand "no." They just run experiments all day long. And your job is to make sure the RIGHT experiments succeed.

[ illustration: puppy running experiments — trying different things to see what works ]

You are training them 24/7

Even when you don't mean to.

Every time you:

You're saying "yes do that again"
  • Open the crate while they're screaming
  • Pet them while they're jumping
  • Give attention during chaos
  • Give them the treat to shut them up
You're saying "that's how you get things"
  • Wait for calm before opening crate
  • Only pet when four paws on floor
  • Reward the quiet moments
  • Ignore the tantrum, reward the settle
real talk It's like giving a kid a candy bar when they're throwing a tantrum in the grocery store line. If you give them the candy to shut them up... you better believe they're gonna do it again next time. Because it worked.

Before anything else

Before you diagnose behavior, check if their basic needs are met. An under-exercised, under-stimulated puppy is just... a tiny terrorist.

🍗 Food

Fed on schedule? Not hangry?

🚽 Potty

Been outside recently? Accidents = you waited too long.

🏃 Exercise

Walked? Played? Burned energy?

🧠 Brain

Mental stimulation today? Tired brain > tired body.

😴 Sleep

Puppies need 18-20 hrs of sleep. Overtired = MORE crazy, not less.

🧸 Chew

Have they had something appropriate to chew on?

golden rule If needs aren't met, behavior problems aren't behavior problems. They're just a puppy telling you something. Meet the need first, then see what's left.

Emotional regulation

This is the part no one teaches.

Your puppy has zero chill and zero coping skills. So you have to teach them. And honestly? It's the exact same skill we're all trying to learn for ourselves lol.

[ illustration: puppy chaos → calm transition ]

The cry-it-out protocol

When they go full psycho (zoomies, barking, chaos):

  1. Ask: did they get what they need? (food, potty, play, brain)
  2. If yes → calmly crate them
  3. Let them cry it out. Do not open that door.
  4. Wait for them to settle (even 2 seconds of quiet counts)
  5. Open the door. Walk away like it's no big deal.
what they learn calm = freedom. Not screaming = freedom.

Calm greetings

When you come home and your puppy is jumping, freaking out, acting like you've been gone for 10 years:

Do not reward that.

Literally just stand there. Wait. The SECOND they calm down → pet them.

Now they learn: being calm gets me attention.

girl... If you squeal "HIIII BABY!!" every time you walk in the door, you're training a jumping machine. Your dog is taking notes.

The On/Off Switch

Crazy hype isn't bad. It's just bad when you can't turn it off. So you build contrast.

🎮 Turn ON the crazy

Pick a phrase: "ARE YOU READY??"

Then play, hype them up, let them go nuts. This is GOOD.

🧊 Turn OFF the crazy

Say: "ENOUGH"

Stop engaging. Go still. Become extremely boring. Robot mode.

Switch between the two. Practice going from hype to calm on command. They learn: fun ends when you say it ends. And starts when you say it starts.

The cheat code: food

Honestly? Just carry kibble around like a crazy person.

Wait for a behavior you like. Throw food at them. That's it. That's the whole technique.

Reward everything you like

  • They come to you randomly → food
  • They sit → food
  • They lay down → food
  • They go in their crate → food
  • They don't jump → food
  • They ignore a dog → food
  • They check in with you on a walk → food

And then in their brains they will be like... "damn HOW DID I GET THAT FOOD. Do I control the food?? What was I doinggg that triggered this yumminess?"

They'll be using their energy to think about how the world works — instead of barking at sounds, chewing things they're not supposed to, and getting into trouble.

pro tip The easiest way to tucker a puppy out is to make them think. A tired brain = angel puppy. A tired body = still chaotic.

Recall: make them love coming back

Don't just yell "come." They don't care.

The GET IT / COME game

When puppies are small, feed them by hand:

  1. Say "GET IT" and throw a piece of kibble across the floor
  2. They run to get it
  3. Say "COME" (or your recall word)
  4. They come back because they know you have more food
  5. Repeat. A lot.

It seems obvious. But repetition like that works. Make it fun, make it a game. They will fall in love with learning.

real talk A lot of people just bark commands at puppies. They don't know what the heck you're saying. They're just like "LALALALA how does the world work, treats are YUMMY, WTF is that!?" Your job is to show them the world through the lens you want them to experience it.

Stop giving them what they want for free

This is where most people mess up. If they really want something, make them earn it by being calm.

🚪 The door

Puppy freaking out to go outside? Don't open it. Wait for calm. THEN open.

calm = door opens

🏠 The crate

Screaming = nothing happens. Calm = door opens. (Unless they need to pee real bad.)

calm = freedom

🦮 The leash

Pulling = we stop. Loose leash = we move. Being dragged by a 12 lb dictator is optional.

calm = progress

Think of it like a passive aggressive teacher waiting for the class to settle down before we can get started.

Your dog is not the problem. Your inconsistency is.
